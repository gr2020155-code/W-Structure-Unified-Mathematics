## 1. The Concept of the Seam ($W$)

In classical geometry, space is treated as an open container where coordinates can diverge to infinity ($\infty$).
In **W-Structure**, divergence is a process, not a place. The logical closure of this process requires a single topological point that binds all directions together.

We define the space $X_W$ as the one-point compactification of the unbounded manifold $\mathcal{U}$:
$$X_W = \mathcal{U} \cup \{W\}$$

**Definition:** The Seam ($W$) is the unique limit point for any sequence $x_n$ such that $|x_n| \to \text{"infinity"}$.

---

## 2. Global Connectivity

Since $W$ is a single point belonging to the manifold:
1.  **Closed Geodesics:** A "straight line" is simply a circle of maximal radius passing through $W$.
2.  **Intersection of Parallels:** Two parallel trajectories attempting to maintain constant separation will eventually meet at the Seam.
    * *Classical view:* Parallels never meet.
    * *W-view:* Parallels meet at the closure point of the coordinate system.

## 3. The Point-Field Duality

Approaching the Seam changes the nature of an object.
* **Locally ($x \ll W$):** An object behaves as a discrete particle (Delta function $\delta_x$).
* **Globally ($x \to W$):** As distance decreases to zero in the inverse chart ($y=1/x$), the object spreads along the topology of the Seam, becoming a global field ($\Phi_W$).

> **Genesis Law:** Space cannot be "infinite" in a physical sense because infinite information requires infinite energy. The Seam is the energetic limit of the system.
